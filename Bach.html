Johann Sebastian Bach was born on March 31, 1685, in Eisenach, a town in Thuringia, Germany. He died on July 28, 1750, in Leipzig, a city in the same region.

Johann Sebastian Bach was a German composer and musician of the Baroque period, born on March 31, 1685, in Eisenach, Thuringia. He was born into a musical family and became a choirboy at the age of nine, receiving his early musical training from his father, Johann Ambrosius Bach, and his uncle, Johann Christoph Bach.

As a young man, Bach held various musical positions, including organist at churches in Arnstadt and Mühlhausen, court musician in Weimar, and music director in Köthen. He also composed a large body of music, including sacred and secular works, chamber music, orchestral suites, and keyboard music, such as his famous Well-Tempered Clavier.
